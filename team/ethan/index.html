I am a graduate student in the Department of Biology at MIT, jointly advised by Eliezer Calo and Aviv Regev at the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard. My interests include RNA localization, spatial transcriptomics, single-cell technologies, the mysteries of the non-coding genome, computational biology, and, more generally, how nature encodes information.

Before coming to MIT, I received a B.S. in Computational Neuroscience and a B.A. in History and Philosophy of Science from the University of Pittsburgh. I've spent time working on de novo transcriptome assembly at the University of Connecticut , signal processing in single neuron recordings in the Yates Lab at Pitt, long read sequencing tech development and in silico simulations in the McCombie Lab at Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory, and writing a pipeline to call enhancers and other regulatory features from NGS datasets in the Gerstein Lab at Yale .
