Arish Shah

I joined the Calo lab in 2017 as part of the first set of graduate students to enter the lab. My interests include ribosome function, translation regulation, developmental biology (particularly zebrafish), the intricacies of early embryogenesis, and new genome-engineering technologies.

Before coming to MIT, I received a B.S. in Bioengineering/Bioinformatics and a B.A. in Art History from the University of California, San Diego. I then spent some time working at Illumina writing software that sits onboard the MiSeq for analysis of short RNA reads. Then worked as a research technician in the Moens Lab at the Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center in Seattle where I worked on developing genome-engineering technologies in the zebrafish and then using those CRISPR-based methods for electrical synapse biology and melanoma metastasis.

When I'm not in lab you can find me , perusing Boston museums, baking French pastries, or drinking local craft beer at Aeronaut.
