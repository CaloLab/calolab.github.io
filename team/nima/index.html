I am a third-year graduate student in MIT Biology, and I am interested in the processes that allow for biological information to be encoded, transmitted, and its expression regulated. I am currently exploring the mechanisms underlying the process of localized translation, which allows for proteins to be synthesized in specific regions of the cell. More specifically, I am using biochemical and molecular genetic approaches to elucidate the mechanisms used by motor proteins to regulate the localization and translation of mRNAs.

I did my undergraduate work in Molecular Genetics at the University of Toronto, where I worked on how the RNA-binding protein Smaug regulates the translation and stability of its mRNA targets in the early Drosophila embryo.

Outside of lab, you can often find me in a nearby rock-climbing gym, cheering for the Toronto Raptors (this is the year!), or reading cool science.
